Divisibility Rules

Divisibility by 2 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 2 if its last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, or 8).
Example: 146 is divisible by 2 because the last digit is 6.


Divisibility by 3 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 3 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 3.
Example: 123 is divisible by 3 because the sum of its digits (1 + 2 + 3 = 6) is divisible by 3.


Divisibility by 4 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 4 if the number formed by its last two digits is divisible by 4.
Example: 3128 is divisible by 4 because the last two digits form the number 28, which is divisible by 4.


Divisibility by 5 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 5 if its last digit is 0 or 5.
Example: 645 is divisible by 5 because the last digit is 5.


Divisibility by 6 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 6 if it is divisible by both 2 and 3.
Example: 132 is divisible by 6 because it is divisible by both 2 (last digit 2 is even) and 3 (sum of digits 1 + 3 + 2 = 6, which is divisible by 3).

Divisibility by 8 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 8 if the number formed by its last three digits is divisible by 8.
Example: 1056 is divisible by 8 because the last three digits, 056 (or simply 56), are divisible by 8.


Divisibility by 9 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 9 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 9.
Example: 2349 is divisible by 9 because the sum of its digits (2 + 3 + 4 + 9 = 18) is divisible by 9.


Divisibility by 10 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 10 if its last digit is 0.
Example: 570 is divisible by 10 because the last digit is 0.


Divisibility by 11 :

Rule: A number is divisible by 11 if the difference between the sum of its digits in odd positions and the sum of its digits in even positions is 0 or divisible by 11.
Example: 2728 is divisible by 11 because the sum of the digits in odd positions (2+2=4) and the sum of the digits in even positions (7 + 8 = 15) have a difference of 11 (15 - 4 = 11), which is divisible by 11.
